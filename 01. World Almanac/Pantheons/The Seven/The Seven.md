The Seven, sometimes known as the Old Gods are older than the old.
The origin of the Seven, and the creation of the world is known only to them.
But the stories go, that the great Mountain, Sléibhte na nDéithe.

Bríonna: Goddess of Birth and Fertility

Nemainne: Goddess of the Fates

Cathmórra: Goddess of Death, Battle and the final quiet.

Osgaire: God of Dreams and Trickery

Riarann: God of Labour and Craft

Múirath: God of the Sea

Aelthir: God of the Air

At the center of the world rises [[Sléibhte na nDéithe]], the Mountain of the Gods, crowned in cloud and silence. On its highest peak, **Aelthir**, Lord of Air, sits in stillness, watching the wind carve the sky.

At its base, **Riarann the Smith** stokes the eternal forge— ploughs for the field, swords for the blood-soaked soil. Beside him, **Bríonna** tends the roots of life, while Cathmórra sharpens war’s edge, sowing death where crops once grew.

From the mountain’s heart flows the Abhann Mór, its source sacred, its path winding. **Múirath**, god of the sea, swims its length, carrying mountain whispers to the ocean’s roar.

High above, **Nemainne** plays the song of life on her Golden Harp, she plucks the chords of birth and death, a song whoes harmonies weave together the fate of all. And when night falls, **Osgáire** cloaks the world in dreams, his clouds drifting like riddles across the moon.
